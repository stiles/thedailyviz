I’m a big fan of Google Correlate, a service that lets you spot real-word trends through Internet searches. A friend this morning told me about a new feature allowing users to draw distributions on a line graph — and then see correlated searches. 

For example, in this graph, I drew a 45-degree decline (seen as a blue line). Google then charts search traffic (red line) for a specific term that’s similar over time. Here, you can see how people have stopped searching for “flash movie” since 2004.
