Last week the U.S. Census Bureau released updated national population estimates, including a list of the counties that grew most rapidly from 2010 to last summer. Included in the release was a note that six more counties had flipped to "minority majority," as the bureau calls them. These are counties in which non-Hispanic whites represent less than half the population.
