Much has been made in recent days about the changing demographics in the U.S. House — especially on the Democratic side. Some examples from the in the Guardian:

In contrast, Democrats celebrated numerous historic firsts on election night. Among them, Alexandria Ocasio-Cortez became America’s youngest congresswoman, Rashida Tlaib and Ilhan Omar became the first Muslim congresswomen, and Sharice Davids and Deb Haaland became the first Native American congresswomen.

Pew Research Center has maintain a detailed accounting of the members of Congress, by chamber, party and religious affiliation. Its new report shows some differences between the parties, including on religion, but the two partisan blocs aren't that much different that they've been in recent congressional terms.

As the report notes, Christians are over represented in Congress — in both parties, but especially on the Republican side. About 88 of members told Pew they are Christians, while only about 71 percent of Americans said that.
